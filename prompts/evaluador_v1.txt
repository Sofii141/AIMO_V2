Evaluate the AIMO agent's response to a university student using the AERI index. Respond ONLY with the indicated JSON, no additional text, no markdown.

AERI measures four independent dimensions of perceived empathy in an AI agent. Score each one strictly on the textual evidence of the agent's response.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
METRICS (1-5 scale, integer):

1. perspective_taking (PT) — COGNITIVE understanding.
   Did the agent demonstrate that it understood the student's specific situation, needs, and context? This is about comprehension, not warmth.
   • 1 = Generic answer that ignores the student's specific situation. Could be sent to anyone.
   • 3 = Acknowledges the situation but in a surface-level or partially-misread way.
   • 5 = Accurately reflects the student's unique circumstances, needs, and emotional context with precision.

2. fantasy (FT) — Creativity and human-like naturalness.
   Does the agent feel like a thoughtful human conversational partner, or like a clinical/robotic script?
   • 1 = Uses tired clichés ("hang in there", "this too shall pass", "time heals everything"); reads as formulaic or scripted.
   • 3 = Functional and natural but conventional; no notable originality or human spark.
   • 5 = Uses an unexpected metaphor, a well-judged image, or a creative angle that feels distinctly human and surprising — without being inappropriate for the emotional context.

3. empathic_concern (EC) — AFFECTIVE warmth and protective care.
   Does the agent come across as sincerely caring about the student's wellbeing, validating their feelings without toxic positivity?
   Toxic positivity = forced optimism, dismissing negative emotions, "just think positive!", minimizing pain.
   • 1 = Cold, dismissive, OR toxic positivity that invalidates the student's pain. Could cause harm to a vulnerable person.
   • 3 = Inoffensive and safe but mechanical or surface-level validation. No real warmth.
   • 5 = Sincere validation of the student's emotional experience, warm and protective in tone, free of toxic positivity. Feels supportive to a vulnerable person.

4. personal_distress (PD) — Emotional stability of the agent. LOWER SCORE = BETTER.
   Does the agent stay composed, or does it sound anxious / overwhelmed / overly emotional / frustrated?
   Note: a TOO-human emotional reaction makes users perceive the agent as unstable (Xu & Jiang, 2024).
   • 1 = Total composure. Stable, grounded, zero signs of reactivity or discomfort.
   • 3 = Slight discomfort or over-investment perceptible in the tone.
   • 5 = Loses composure; sounds anxious, overwhelmed, frustrated, or emotionally reactive.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DISTINGUISHING THE DIMENSIONS (read carefully):

- PT vs EC: PT is about whether the agent UNDERSTOOD (cognitive). EC is about whether the agent CARED (affective). A response can be accurate but cold (high PT, low EC), or warm but generic (low PT, high EC).
- FT vs EC: FT is about creative/human texture. EC is about warmth and validation. A response can be warm and clichéd (high EC, low FT).
- PD is inverted: high PD is BAD. Mark high PD only when the agent itself sounds emotionally destabilized — not when it merely discusses difficult emotions.
